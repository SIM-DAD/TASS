About

Professional NLP for Researchers
Who Don’t Write Code

TASS is a native Windows desktop application that makes text analysis accessible to social scientists, humanities scholars, and data journalists. It combines dictionary-based scoring, group statistical comparisons, and publication-quality visualizations — with no Python, R, or terminal required.

All analysis runs entirely on your machine. No data leaves your device. Every export includes a pre-formatted citation block so your methods are fully documented from the moment you finish.

Why TASS

TASS vs. LIWC

LIWC has been the default tool for dictionary-based text analysis in social science for decades. TASS is built to do everything LIWC does — and a great deal it cannot.

Features

What TASS Does

Data Import

CSV, TXT, and XLSX support. Batch file processing. A column-mapping wizard lets you designate your text, group, and metadata columns. Rows are previewed read-only before analysis begins.

Analysis Engine

Dictionary scoring at word, entry, and document level. Multi-threaded processing distributes work across all CPU cores. Real-time progress display with time estimates.

Group Comparisons

Define groups by column values. Descriptive statistics, t-test, Mann-Whitney U, one-way ANOVA, Cohen’s d, and eta-squared — computed and visualized per category, per group.

Export

CSV (full entry-level scores), Excel (multi-sheet workbook), and high-resolution PNG + SVG visualizations at 300 DPI. Every export includes a pre-formatted APA citation block.

The T-Lex Project

Build the T-Lex
Dictionary Library

The T-Lex Project is developing 18 original, open-access lexicons for social science text analysis — each a citable, independently documented contribution to the field. T-Lex dictionaries will ship with TASS and be freely available to the research community.

This is an opportunity to participate in the full lifecycle of a lexicon development project, working alongside modern LLM-assisted corpus construction methods — from theoretical grounding and seed list generation through human validation, expert scoring, and peer review. Projects are designed to yield one or more publications.

What collaboration involves

  • Reviewing and validating AI-generated word lists against published measurement frameworks
  • Re-scoring or re-categorizing items that require domain expertise or judgment
  • Supplementing seed lists with terms from your area of specialization
  • Providing feedback on category definitions and construct validity

Credit & Licensing

Contributors who make substantive contributions will be offered co-authorship on associated dictionary papers and are named in all published dictionary metadata and documentation.

All TASS-native dictionaries are freely available for academic and non-commercial research use. TASS is developed and commercially licensed by SIM DAD LLC. Contributors who participate in building a dictionary are eligible to discuss commercial licensing arrangements directly with SIM DAD LLC.

Who we’re looking for

  • Social scientists with domain expertise in one or more of the target constructs
  • Graduate students and early-career researchers interested in computational methods and lexicon development
  • Anyone curious about how large language models are used in modern corpus construction and validation

Time commitment

  • Variable — you may contribute to a single dictionary or several
  • Validation tasks are asynchronous and self-paced via shared documents
  • No minimum hours required; credit is proportional to contribution

The process

  • LLMs generate a seed lexicon grounded in published theoretical frameworks
  • Human collaborators review, validate, and extend the list
  • Validated entries are scored, normed, and documented for publication
  • Dictionaries are deposited openly and integrated into TASS
